Top 5 Classic Barbie Movies

Anneliese (left) & Erika (right)

#1: Princess and the Pauper

Music: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Story: ★ ★ ★ ★
Costume: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Villain: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Princess and the Pauper is a very popular Barbie movie with two of the most recognizable characters in Barbie history: Anneliese and Erika. The music in this film is clever, beautiful, and even humorous, and its villain is preposterous in the best way possible. If you could be recommended one Barbie movie and one Barbie movie only, this is it!

Princess Genevieve (pink dress) & Her 12 Sisters

#2: The 12 Dancing Princesses

Music: ★ ★ ★  
Story: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Costume: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Villain: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★

Barbie in the 12 Dancing Princess is a more underrated Barbie movie, but it is certainly one of the most golden. In this movie, the story line and villain are show-stopping. Arguably some of the best we've seen in Barbie history, not to mention that the colors and symbolism of the costumes are genius! Unfortunately, this movie doesn't have any singing, but the theme song happens to be one of the most iconic songs. 

Ro & Prince Antonio

#3: The Island Princess

 Music: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Story: ★ ★ 
Costume: ★ ★ ★ ★ 
Villain: ★ ★ ★ ★ 



Barbie as the Island Princess, while having several dull characters, has the greatest duet in Barbie history: I Need to Know. It does have an interesting villain as well as many fun and diverse animal characters. As for costume, I have always found Ro's ball dress, as displayed in the image, stunning. Although, I certainly think it could be improved if the feathers were lying down against her skirt rather than sticking up. Over all, the plot was interesting enough, though it certainly can't top #1 and #2.

Rapunzel in Her Tower

#4 Rapunzel

Music: ★ ★ ★
Story: ★ ★ ★ 
Costume: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Villain: ★ ★ ★ ★

Barbie Rapunzel, just like 12 Dancing Princesses, doesn't really have any singing. There is one lullaby, but that is all. Luckily, this movie has an artistic twist on the original Rapunzel tale. In this rendition, Rapunzel's hairbrush turns into a magic paintbrush and leads her on many adventures (and creates some fun fashion pieces as well). Though the royals we see aren't too bright, Gothel is very despicable and makes the movie exciting!

Odette

#5 Swan Lake

Music: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Story: ★ ★ ★ ★
Costume: ★ ★ ★ ★ ★
Villain: ★ ★ ★

Barbie Swan Lake is another song-less feature, but it does feature dancing, magic, and cute animals, so naturally it ranks highly. The beautiful costumes and sassy creatures paired with Tchaikovsky's beautiful Swan Lake score, make for a great watch! Rothbart and his daughter Odile are full of character and magic and help produce some good old fashioned suspense. 

Clara After Her Dress Transformation

#6 The Nutcracker

Music: ★ ★ ★ ★
Story: ★ ★
Costume: ★ ★ ★ ★ 
Villain: ★ ★ ★ ★

Barbie Nutcracker is such a classic film! It heavily impacted my childhood with some great memories. Of course the music is fantastic since Tchaikovsky himself composed it. Unfortunately, they paired the wrongs songs with the wrong sort of scenes, which now gets on my nerves after performing in the Nutcracker many times. Also, the prince is not my favorite of all the princes, not to mention that the romance moves too quickly, but the mouse king is hilarious as well as his side kick: a bat. At the end, we see a fabulous outfit change for Clara with a glamorous pink romantic tutu, long golden curls, and a tiara. Worth a watch, but definitely not as strong as the other picks.
